Fun Facts About Tortoiseshell Cats

Tortoiseshell does not refer to the breed, but the colors of their fur.

The tortoiseshell coloring can occur in nearly all breeds of cats.

Over 99% of tortie cats are female due to their fur colors being a result of rare genetic combinations.

If their fur colors appear woven together it is called "bridled". If they appear in large sections it is called "patched".

While it has not been scientifically proven, torties are known for having an attitude, or "toritude."

In Ireland, torties are thought to bring good luck. In Japan they are thought to protect against bad storms, shipwrecks, and ghosts.

Chimera torties feature orange on one side of their body and black on the other, making them appear two faced.

Poppy can be described as a "caliby", or a tortoiseshell with a white undercoat.
